**Knots 1 **

<table>
  <tr>
    <td><strong>Square Knot
</strong><br/>
<br/>
<br/>
<br/>
<br/>
<br/>
<br/>Left over right
<br/>Go around
<br/>Right over left
<br/>Go around
<br/>Pull tight
<br/>
<br/><strong>Timber Hitch</strong><img alt="image" src="inline-object:kix.6zgisvu8clyr"/>
<br/>Pass the rope around the log or object.
<br/>Bring the working end around the standing part.
<br/>Pass the working end around itself once so it forms a loop.
<br/>Wrap the working end tightly around the standing part 3?4 times.
<br/>Pull the standing part to snug the wraps; the hitch tightens under load.
<br/>(Optional) Add a half hitch or two on the standing part for extra security.
</td>
    <td><strong>Clove Hitch
</strong><br/>Make an X on the pole
<br/>Put the end of the rope under the X and pull tight
<br/>
<br/><strong>Bowline
</strong><br/><img alt="image" src="inline-object:kix.s5xw6v4nfd5t"/>
<br/>Make a loop
<br/>Go through the hole
<br/>Go around the non looped part
<br/>Go back through the first hole
<br/>Pull tight
<br/>
<br/><strong>Two Half Hitches
</strong><br/>Wrap the rope around a rod
<br/>Pass the end through the loop
<br/>Wrap it around the taut part, looping into itself
<br/>Pull tight
</td>

**Knots 2**

<table>
  <tr>
    <td><strong>Taut-Line Hitch</strong><img alt="image" src="inline-object:kix.ixdbbgu7soy5"/><strong>
</strong><br/>Wrap the rope around a pole and make a loop
<br/>Make 2 inside wraps and 1 outside wrap
<br/>Pull tight then slide the knot along the standing part to adjust tension
<br/>
<br/><strong>Sheet Bend
</strong><br/><img alt="image" src="inline-object:kix.a332snpl9yb8"/>
<br/>Make a loop with the thick rope, and put the thin rope through it
<br/>Bring the thin rope under and around
<br/>Put the thin rope under itself and pull tight
</td>
    <td><strong>Trucker?s Hitch</strong>
<br/><img alt="image" src="inline-object:kix.f6o738p6cdat"/>
<br/>Tie a small slip knot in the middle of the rope
<br/>Run the working end around the anchor point.
<br/>Pass it through the loop to make a pulley.
<br/>Pull tight to tension the line.
<br/>Secure with two Half Hitches.
<br/>
<br/><strong>Trucker?s Hitch - Tips
</strong><br/>Instead of the carabiner it can be any cylindrical support like a tree trunk, etc.
<br/>It can be finished off with a taut line hitch. Less slipping than the latter.
<br/>Because the variations of the hitch use the loop in the standing part and the anchor point as pulleys, it gives a mechanical advantage of 2:1 if you&#39;re pulling at the working end.
<br/>The knot can be secured with a double half hitch on one/both lines
</td>
  </tr>
</table>

**Knives 1 **

Demonstrate how to safely pass a knife both fixed blade and folding \(can be done around a circle\)

Explain how to properly clean a knife \(soap and water, oiling, caring for wooden handles, etc.\)

Demonstrate how to properly sharpen and strop a knife.

Demonstrate how to properly use a knife to cut and carve \(cut away, safety circle?\)

Needs: One instructor and several older scouts or adults to supervise, sharpening stones, extra pocket knives, a kitchen knife.











**Knives 2 **

Demonstrate how to properly pass an axe, hatchet and hand saw.

Demonstrate how to properly store and maintain a hatchet and saw.

Demonstrate how to properly sharpen an axe or hatchet.

Demonstrate how to properly ?size? an axe.

Demonstrate proper technique using an axe, hatchet and hand saw.



When done, discuss the outdoor code.



Needs: One instructor, two helpers \(to monitor axe sharpening\), rope and support to set up an axe yard, supplemental lighting, hatchets, an axe, handsaws, files.  Additional supplies may include a maul and wedge \(for show, not for active teaching at this time\).
